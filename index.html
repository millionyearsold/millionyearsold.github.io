Welcome to Lego Adelphi

Here you will find (hopefully) useful instructions and guidance for building the Lego Adelphi.

The Lego Adelphi is a small representation of the real building in London but made from about 980 pieces of lego instead of traditional
construction materials. There are many benefits to using lego including:

  • Lego is relatively inexpensive
  • It's small and so easily transportable
  • Construction time is significantly reduced
  • There's no shortage of skilled labour
  • Lego buildings have a low eco footprint

Please note that there is a slight excess of building materials included with your package. This is to allow for even more artistic license
and contingency (e.g. in the event that additional stability is required due to lack of structural integrity).

Step-by-Step Instructions

Step by step instructions provide the most detailed web based Lego Adelphi building guidance but there are some limitations. Most notably transparent
building materials are invisible in the item list for each page. Another curiosity is that building process is not the same
as was used during the design phase. It's possible that this could make some of the components more difficult
to complete - particularly internal components in the latter stages of the build.

Visual Guide

The visual guide is a series of images showing the Lego Adelphi in various stages of design from start to finish. It is intended to complement the step-by-step instructions. Be aware that there was a system outage on the 8th/9th floor video screen representation during design phase. This has been rectified for the building phase - the video screen now shows the latest fashion news.

Warning: the Lego Adelphi has not been designed to comply with building regulations. There have been no surveys. The HSE has not
been consulted or even informed (and as far as we know they are completely unaware of the existence of the Lego Adelphi).
